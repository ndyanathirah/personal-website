The Weight of Life

Some days the weight feels far too much, The world too loud, too soft, too rough. The sun may shine, but clouds still cling, Constant pull, an endless swing.

The heart is heavy, the mind unsure, As dreams dissolve and fears endure. I reach for peace, but it slips away, Lost in the noise, too far to stay.

The path is long, the road is steep, And sometimes I just want to sleep. But even in the darkest night, I find the strength to keep the fight.

For struggle shapes the soul, they say, And teaches me to find my way. In every tear, a lesson’s learned, In every loss, a hope returned.

So though the storm may rage and roar, I’ll rise again, and rise once more. For life is tough, but so am I, And I’ll keep reaching for the sky.
